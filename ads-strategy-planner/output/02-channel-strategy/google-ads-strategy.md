# Google Ads Strategy

## Role in Media Mix

Google Ads is the primary **intent capture** engine:
- Search and Shopping run from day 1.
- Performance Max and Demand Gen are used for expansion after stability.

## Campaign Architecture

| Campaign Type | Primary Role | Funnel Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Search | Capture high-intent demand | Bottom |
| Shopping | Product-level intent conversion | Bottom |
| Performance Max | AI-assisted discovery and scaling | Mid |
| Demand Gen | Awareness and assisted conversion | Top |

## Launch Sequence

1. Launch Search + Shopping.
2. Reach minimum stability threshold (30 conversions in 30 days).
3. Add Performance Max with strict exclusions.
4. Add Demand Gen when CAC/CPC pressure rises and scale is needed.

## Optimization Standards

- Use intent themes per ad group (Broad + Exact control anchor).
- Add negative keyword themes weekly (informational, navigational, irrelevant services).
- Keep bidding stable; avoid frequent strategy changes.
- Optimize for profit metrics, not CTR in isolation.

## Budget and Bidding Guardrails

- Smart bidding is default (`Maximize Conversions`, `tCPA`, or `tROAS`).
- Daily budget baseline: target enough spend for at least 10 clicks/day per active segment.
- Apply a 60-day learning protection period before major bid resets.

## Measurement Priorities

- Enhanced Conversions and GTM setup are mandatory.
- For lead gen, implement offline conversion imports.
- Track lagging and leading indicators: CVR, CPA, contribution margin, and brand search lift.

## Key Risks and Mitigations

- **Risk:** Low-quality broad match expansion  
  **Mitigation:** Exact-match anchors plus weekly negatives.
- **Risk:** PMax cannibalizes branded demand  
  **Mitigation:** Brand exclusions and query governance.
- **Risk:** Early over-optimization  
  **Mitigation:** Enforce minimum data windows before major decisions.
