# LinkedIn Ads Strategy

## Role in Media Mix

LinkedIn Ads is the primary **B2B audience quality** and **professional demand shaping** channel:
- Use for ICP precision, lead quality, and event/content-driven pipeline support.

## Funnel Structure

| Stage | Objective | Recommended Formats |
|---|---|---|
| TOFU | Reach new ICP accounts and stakeholders | Sponsored Content, Video Ads |
| MOFU | Retarget engagers and site visitors | Carousel Ads, Thought Leader Ads |
| BOFU | Convert qualified prospects | Lead Gen Forms, Event Ads, Message Ads |

## Targeting Strategy

- Segment by job title, function, seniority, company size, and industry.
- Build audience cohorts by funnel intent (cold, warm, hot).
- Sync CRM signals to suppress non-fit leads and prioritize high-intent segments.

## Creative and Offer Strategy

- Promote educational assets and proof-based offers (case studies, webinars, demos).
- Use native feed creative with clear business outcomes and social proof.
- Keep message continuity from ad to form/page to improve conversion quality.

## Operating Cadence

- Weekly: review CPL, lead quality, and pipeline influence by segment.
- Bi-weekly: creative refresh and offer sequencing updates.
- Monthly: reallocate budget toward highest quality cohorts.

## Key Risks and Mitigations

- **Risk:** High CPL without pipeline quality  
  **Mitigation:** Tight qualification fields in Lead Gen Forms + CRM scoring.
- **Risk:** Fatigue in narrow ICP audiences  
  **Mitigation:** Rotate creatives and expand adjacent persona pools.
- **Risk:** Volume bias over quality  
  **Mitigation:** Optimize to qualified pipeline contribution, not lead count alone.
