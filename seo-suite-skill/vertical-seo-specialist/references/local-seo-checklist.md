# Local SEO Checklist & Citation Management

*Sourced from: The Art of SEO — Enge, Spencer, Fishkin & Stricchiola (Ch. 8: Optimizing for Vertical Search)*

## Local SEO Master Checklist

### Foundation: Data Aggregators

Fix the source data first — errors here propagate to hundreds of downstream directories.

```
□ InfoUSA — verify/correct business name, address, phone
□ Acxiom — verify/correct business name, address, phone
□ Amacai — verify/correct business name, address, phone
```

### Direct Listing Verification (Search Engines)

These platforms accept data directly from the business and treat it as highest-trust:

```
□ Google Business Profile — claimed, verified, fully completed
□ Bing Places for Business — claimed and verified
□ Apple Maps Connect — claimed and verified
□ Yahoo! Local — claimed and updated
□ Yelp for Business — claimed, photos added, review responses set up
```

### Yellow Pages and Major Directories

```
□ YellowPages.com
□ Superpages.com
□ CitySearch
□ Judy's Book
□ Whitepages.com
□ Local newspaper directory (check top papers in your metro area)
□ Chamber of commerce membership listing (request linked mention)
□ BBBOnLine (if member)
□ Industry-specific vertical directories (identify top 3–5 for your niche)
□ Specialty demographic directories (language, interest, industry — if applicable)
```

### On-Site Local Signals

```
□ Title tags include: [Business Name] + [Business Type] + [City, State]
   Example: "Acme Café: French Restaurant in Boston, MA"
□ H1 tag on location page includes location name
□ Full NAP (Name, Address, Phone) in crawlable HTML text in footer or Contact page
   — NOT in an image, NOT in JavaScript only
□ hCard microformat applied to NAP on Contact page
□ Each location has its own dedicated page (not one page listing all locations)
□ Each location page includes: map embed, hours, services, photos, "since X" info
□ Location-distinguishing details: neighborhood, street, area nicknames
```

### Review Management

```
□ Process in place for asking satisfied customers to leave reviews
□ Monitoring set up for new reviews on: Google, Yelp, CitySearch, Superpages, YP.com
□ Policy for responding to negative reviews (respond within 48 hours)
□ No fake reviews, no incentivized reviews, no competitor review manipulation
```

### Local Link Building

```
□ Chamber of commerce membership — request linked listing
□ Local charity event sponsorships — request linked sponsor mention
□ Local school/college sports team sponsorships — request linked mention on school site
□ Local news site features or business profiles
□ Local fraternal organization listings (Lions Club, Rotary, etc.)
□ Community events hosted or sponsored by the business — pages hosted on your domain
□ Feedmap added to local blog (feedmap.net)
```

## NAP Consistency Rules

**NAP = Name, Address, Phone**

Every instance of your business information across the web must be byte-for-byte identical. Even minor formatting differences create conflicting signals.

| ❌ Inconsistent | ✓ Consistent |
|---|---|
| "Acme Café" vs "Acme Cafe" | Pick one — use it everywhere |
| "39 Temple St" vs "39 Temple Street" | Pick one abbreviation style |
| "Suite 400" vs "Ste 400" vs "#400" | Pick one format |
| "(617) 555-0100" vs "617-555-0100" | Pick one phone format |
| "Boston, MA 02101" vs "Boston MA 02101" | Include comma consistently |
| "www.acmecafe.com" vs "acmecafe.com" | Include protocol consistently |

**Audit process:**
1. Define your canonical NAP — the exact string that will appear everywhere
2. Search Google for your business name and review the top 20+ listings
3. Use BrightLocal, Whitespark, or Moz Local to surface all citation instances
4. Update every inconsistency to match the canonical version
5. Re-audit quarterly — aggregators push updates that can overwrite your corrections

## Multi-Location Business Management

For chains, franchises, or businesses with 10+ locations:

### Manual management (up to ~10 locations)
- Create individual location pages on the website for each outlet
- Claim and verify each location separately in Google Business Profile, Bing, and Apple Maps
- Maintain a citation tracking spreadsheet per location

### Feed-based management (10–100+ locations)
- Contact top aggregators (InfoUSA, Acxiom, Amacai) directly to supply a periodic bulk feed
- Contact major Yellow Pages publishers directly for bulk data submissions
- Use a local listing management service:
  - **Localeze** — aggregator-level distribution
  - **Yext** — direct publisher network (different from aggregator model)
  - **BrightLocal** — audit and management platform
  - **Moz Local** — citation building and monitoring

### Per-location page template

Each location page should be unique — not a template with just the address swapped:

```
Page title: [Business Name] [City] — [Business Type]
H1: [Business Name] in [Neighborhood/City]

Unique content:
- Full address (crawlable text + hCard markup)
- Phone number
- Hours of operation
- Unique description of this location (staff, specialties, history)
- Services or products specific to this location
- Photos of this location
- Google Maps embed
- Parking / transit information
- Certifications or awards specific to this location
- "In business at this location since [year]"
- Link to other nearby locations (internal link)
```

## City Centroid Reference

Search engines have historically used a "city centroid" — the geographic center of a city — as a proximity reference point for local rankings. Businesses closer to the centroid rank higher for general city-wide searches (e.g., "plumber Boston").

**Current status:** Less dominant as a ranking factor than in the book's publication period (2009), but still a consideration for highly competitive local markets.

**Practical implication:** If evaluating physical office/storefront locations, proximity to the downtown core or geographic center can provide a minor local SEO advantage. This is a secondary consideration — NAP consistency, review volume, and on-page signals are far more impactful.

## Local Search Ranking Factor Priority

Based on the book's guidance, ranked by impact:

1. **User ratings and reviews** — cited as "one of the biggest factors" for Google Maps ranking
2. **NAP consistency** across all directories and your website
3. **Direct verification** with Google Business Profile / Bing Places
4. **On-page local signals** (title tag, H1, address in HTML, location-specific content)
5. **Local-relevant inbound links** (local anchor text, locally-oriented linking domains)
6. **Proximity to city centroid** (less impactful than in the past)
7. **Zip code proximity** (very minor; zip code searches are uncommon)
