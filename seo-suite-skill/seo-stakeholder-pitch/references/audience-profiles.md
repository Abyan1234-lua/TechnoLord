# Audience Profiles: Tailoring the SEO Pitch

Different executives have different priorities. Match the pitch frame to the person.

---

## CEO / Founder

**What they care about:** Growth, competitive position, defensibility, long-term equity
**What they fear:** Being outpaced by competitors, over-reliance on a single channel, wasted investment

**Best pitch frame:** Competitive moat
> "Competitors X and Y have built organic channels that generate an estimated [traffic/revenue]. That took them 2–3 years to build. Every month we don't invest is a month harder to catch up. This is about building a durable, compounding acquisition channel — one that works even when we stop spending."

**What to include:**
- Specific competitor examples with estimated organic value
- Long-term compounding trajectory (not just 90-day results)
- Framing as equity-building vs. rent-paying (SEO = owning, paid = renting)

**What to avoid:**
- Technical SEO details
- Rankings
- Any request that feels experimental or unproven

---

## CMO / VP Marketing

**What they care about:** Efficient CAC, channel diversification, brand authority, pipeline
**What they fear:** Over-reliance on paid, rising CPCs, attribution gaps

**Best pitch frame:** Channel efficiency and diversification
> "Our blended CAC from paid is $X and rising. SEO-acquired customers cost [fraction of that] to acquire at scale. We're currently 90% dependent on paid — that's a channel concentration risk. This investment diversifies our acquisition and reduces CAC long-term."

**What to include:**
- CAC comparison (paid vs. projected organic CAC)
- Funnel contribution: which stages organic traffic influences
- Attribution model — how you'll measure and report SEO's contribution
- Content leverage: SEO content often doubles as sales enablement, email nurture, etc.

**What to avoid:**
- Technical implementation details
- Anything that feels like "more content" without a clear ROI model

---

## CFO / Finance Lead

**What they care about:** ROI, payback period, risk, cash efficiency
**What they fear:** Spending money on things they can't measure, long time-to-return

**Best pitch frame:** Asset vs. expense; bounded investment, long-duration return
> "This is a one-time build with compounding returns. At $[investment], and assuming [conversion rate] and [ACV], payback is [X months]. After that, every organic visit is essentially free. For comparison, we'd need to spend $[Y] in paid to generate equivalent traffic indefinitely."

**What to include:**
- Specific dollar investment
- Conservative return estimate with clear assumptions
- Payback timeline
- "What if we stop?" scenario — show the channel keeps producing
- Comparison to paid CAC at equivalent volume

**What to avoid:**
- Impressions, clicks, or any metric that doesn't tie to revenue
- Long-range hockey-stick projections without backing

---

## VP Product / Head of Product

**What they care about:** User value, roadmap prioritization, engineering ROI
**What they fear:** Requests that eat engineering time without clear user benefit

**Best pitch frame:** Product investment with user value at the center
> "There are [X] people a month searching for [problem our product solves] and finding [competitor or no good result]. We can build something better. SEO is just how those users will discover it. The engineering ask is [specific, scoped]: [X weeks] to build [Y]."

**What to include:**
- User need framing first — not SEO, but the user problem
- Scoped, specific engineering ask (never "ongoing SEO work")
- Clear acceptance criteria (what does done look like?)
- Fit within product processes: show it like any other product spec

**What to avoid:**
- Anything that sounds like "SEO maintenance" or ongoing checklist work
- Vague asks like "we need SEO-friendly infrastructure"
- Framing it as a marketing initiative — keep it in the product frame

---

## Engineering Lead / CTO

**What they care about:** Technical quality, build-once architecture, clean implementation
**What they fear:** Rework, hacky workarounds, scope creep, unclear requirements

**Best pitch frame:** Build it right the first time
> "SEO requirements built into the product from the start are far cheaper than retrofitting later. Here's a specific list of [X] changes that improve both user experience and crawlability. All are one-time changes, no ongoing maintenance."

**What to include:**
- Specific, clearly defined technical requests (not "better SEO")
- User experience benefit alongside SEO benefit — they care about UX
- Ticket-ready format: problem, proposed solution, acceptance criteria
- Small-batch framing: prioritize 3–5 high-impact items over a wishlist

**What to avoid:**
- A long list of SEO "hygiene" items with no business impact attached
- Anything that sounds like ongoing SEO maintenance work
- Jargon they won't recognize (DA, SERP, meta keywords, etc.)

---

## Board / Investors

**What they care about:** Scalable growth, capital efficiency, defensibility
**What they fear:** Growth that doesn't scale, over-dependence on paid

**Best pitch frame:** Scalable, defensible organic growth channel
> "We're investing in building an organic acquisition channel that scales non-linearly with investment. Unlike paid, which requires proportional ongoing spend, a well-built SEO program compounds — traffic grows as content indexes and earns authority. Our plan: [initiative] by [date], targeting [revenue/traffic] by [date]."

**What to include:**
- Capital efficiency story (organic CAC vs. paid CAC)
- Compounding growth curve (with realistic projections)
- Competitive differentiation — who's winning organically and what it's worth
- Milestone-based plan

**What to avoid:**
- Operational detail
- Technical SEO specifics
- Anything that sounds like "we need to fix our website"
