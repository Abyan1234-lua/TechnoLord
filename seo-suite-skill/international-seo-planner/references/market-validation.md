# International Market Validation Worksheet

Use this before committing to any international SEO investment. The goal is to establish whether demand is real, what the competitive landscape looks like, and what the realistic opportunity is worth.

---

- Sessions > 0 with reasonable engagement = latent demand worth investing in
- Sessions > 0 but zero conversions = demand exists but site experience or product availability is the barrier (fix that first)
- Sessions ≈ 0 = no organic foothold yet; demand needs to be validated via keyword research before committing

---

## Step 2: Google Search Console query analysis

Filter GSC performance data by target country. Export and review:

- What queries are generating impressions from that country?
- What's the average position for those queries?
- Are there queries in the target language (not just English)?

**Green light:** Non-English impressions already appearing, positions 20–50 (not ranking, but Google knows you're relevant)
**Yellow light:** English impressions only, no local language queries
**Red light:** Zero impressions from that country after 6+ months of operation

**What you're looking for:**
- Are local players dominating? If yes, expect a long runway to compete.
- Are global brands (like your site) ranking? If yes, your .com has a shot.
- Is the SERP mostly local-language content? If yes, you need local-language content to compete.
- Is the SERP thin or low-quality? If yes, the opportunity is real and accessible.

## Step 5: Go / no-go criteria

Check all that apply:

**Strong go signals:**
- [ ] Existing analytics traffic from target country
- [ ] Non-English impressions in GSC
- [ ] Product is available and usable in that market
- [ ] Team has capacity to maintain localized content ongoing
- [ ] Market size estimate justifies investment
- [ ] Competitors are present but beatable

**Pause signals (fix these first):**
- [ ] Product not yet available in target country
- [ ] No capacity for ongoing content maintenance
- [ ] Site has significant technical SEO issues that haven't been fixed domestically
- [ ] No native speaker available for content review

**Hard stop signals:**
- [ ] Product legally unavailable in target country
- [ ] Market research shows no product-market fit
- [ ] Budget is insufficient to do this properly (half-measures in international SEO waste money)
