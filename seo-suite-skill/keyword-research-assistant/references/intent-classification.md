# Intent Classification Reference

Search intent is the most important filter in keyword research. A keyword cannot rank if the page doesn't satisfy the intent behind it — regardless of how well the page is technically optimised.

---

## The four intent types

### 1. Informational intent
**User goal:** Learn, understand, or research something. Not looking to buy yet.

**Signal phrases:**
- how to, how does, what is, what are, why, when, where
- guide, tutorial, explained, overview, definition, introduction, tips
- "is [concept] possible", "can you", "should I"

**SERP signals (what typically ranks):**
- Long-form guides and blog posts
- Wikipedia / reference articles
- YouTube videos
- Featured snippets with definitions or step lists
- "People Also Ask" boxes dominate

**Best page types:**
- Comprehensive guide
- How-to article
- Explainer / educational post
- FAQ page
- Glossary entry

**Conversion relevance:** Low to medium. User is building knowledge, not looking to transact. However, capturing these users early builds brand recognition and trust.

---

### 2. Navigational intent
**User goal:** Find a specific website, page, or brand they already know.

**Signal phrases:**
- Brand name + "login", "pricing", "homepage", "contact"
- "[Product] download", "[software] install"
- "[Company name] [specific page]"

**SERP signals:**
- Homepage or specific branded page ranks #1
- Sitelinks often appear
- Knowledge panel may appear

**Best page types:**
- Homepage (for brand name searches)
- Login / account page
- Pricing page
- Contact page
- Specific product or feature page

**Conversion relevance:** Very high for your own branded terms. Low opportunity for competitor branded terms (users are looking for a specific site, not alternatives — unless you're explicitly an alternative content page).

---

### 3. Commercial investigation intent
**User goal:** Research and compare options before making a decision. Evaluation mode.

**Signal phrases:**
- best, top, vs, versus, comparison, review, alternative, recommend
- "X or Y", "[product] worth it", "[category] for [audience]"
- "[product] pros and cons", "[product] honest review"

**SERP signals:**
- Review / comparison articles dominate
- Listicles ("Top 10 X for Y")
- Third-party review sites (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot)
- Some product/landing pages

**Best page types:**
- Comparison page (X vs Y)
- "Best X for Y" listicle
- Product category page
- Use case page
- Customer case studies

**Conversion relevance:** High. User is close to a decision. These keywords are often the most valuable in B2B SaaS and high-consideration purchases.

---

### 4. Transactional intent
**User goal:** Take action — buy, sign up, download, book, subscribe.

**Signal phrases:**
- buy, purchase, order, get, sign up, subscribe, download, install, book, hire
- "free trial", "pricing", "coupon", "discount", "deal"
- "[product] free", "[service] near me"

**SERP signals:**
- Product pages and landing pages rank
- Ads almost always present (high commercial intent = high advertiser competition)
- Shopping results (Google Shopping) appear for products
- Local pack for local services

**Best page types:**
- Product / service landing page
- Pricing page
- Sign-up / free trial page
- Booking or contact page
- Location-specific page

**Conversion relevance:** Highest. These users are ready to act. Ranking here drives direct revenue.

---

## Mixed and ambiguous intent

Many keywords have mixed intent — different users searching the same phrase want different things. This is where you must look at the actual SERP to understand what Google has decided the dominant intent is.

**Example:** "project management"
- Some users want to learn what project management is (informational)
- Some users want software (commercial/transactional)
- Google primarily shows software comparison pages and tooling — dominant intent is commercial

**How to classify mixed-intent keywords:**
1. Search the keyword in an incognito window
2. Observe the first 5 organic results — what format are they?
3. Are there ads? (Indicates commercial/transactional)
4. Is there a featured snippet? (Indicates informational)
5. Assign the intent that matches the majority of results

If a keyword's SERP is 60%+ one intent type, classify it as that type. If genuinely split, note it and plan for the content to address both angles.

## Intent classification by SERP feature

| SERP feature present | Likely intent |
|---|---|
| Featured snippet (definition box) | Informational |
| "People Also Ask" box prominent | Informational |
| Knowledge panel | Navigational or informational |
| News carousel at top | Informational (trending/news) |
| Image pack | Informational or commercial visual |
| Video results | Informational (how-to) or entertainment |
| Shopping results | Transactional |
| Local pack (map + 3 businesses) | Local/transactional |
| Ads at top (1–4 ads) | Transactional or commercial |
| Review site aggregators (G2, Capterra, etc.) | Commercial investigation |
| Comparison/listicle blog posts | Commercial investigation |

## Intent and keyword difficulty interaction

Intent affects realistic difficulty independently of the KD score:

**Informational keywords with moderate KD:**
Blog posts and guides can outperform authoritative sites if they're genuinely more helpful and complete. Intent match is high-leverage here.

**Transactional keywords with low KD:**
These can be deceptively hard — you may need product-market fit and trust signals (reviews, social proof) that go beyond on-page optimisation.

**Commercial investigation keywords:**
Ranking here often requires earning placement on third-party review and comparison sites, not just optimising your own page.

**Navigational keywords for competitors:**
Generally not worth targeting as primary keywords. Users who type a competitor's brand name want that specific site. The exception is explicitly labelled comparison or alternative pages.

## Intent-to-page type quick reference

| Intent | Funnel stage | Page type | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Informational | Awareness | Blog post, guide, explainer | Subscribe, read more, download |
| Informational | Awareness | FAQ / glossary | Internal link to deeper content |
| Commercial | Consideration | Comparison post | "See pricing", "Start free trial" |
| Commercial | Consideration | Use case page | "Book a demo", "See case study" |
| Commercial | Consideration | "Best X" listicle | "Try free", "View pricing" |
| Transactional | Decision | Landing page | Sign up, buy, book |
| Transactional | Decision | Pricing page | "Get started", "Contact sales" |
| Navigational | Any | Brand / product page | Whatever is most relevant |
| Local transactional | Decision | Location page | "Call", "Book", "Get directions" |
